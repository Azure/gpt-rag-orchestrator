## Negotiation Scenario: Enterprise SaaS Software Sale

**Product Type**: Cloud-based enterprise software (ERP, CRM, HCM, or similar)
**Typical Deal Size**: $500K - $2M annual contract value
**Sales Cycle**: 3-6 months typical

### Key Value Propositions
- 30% reduction in manual processes through automation
- Real-time analytics and reporting dashboards
- Seamless integration with existing systems (SAP, Salesforce, etc.)
- 99.9% uptime SLA with enterprise-grade security
- Dedicated customer success manager

### Buyer Context
- Company is actively evaluating 3-4 competing vendors
- Current system is 8-12 years old and showing limitations
- Budget has been approved but is competitive
- Implementation timeline: 6 months preferred, 9 months maximum
- Key stakeholders: IT, Finance, Operations, and end users

### Common Objections to Expect
1. **Migration Risk**: "What about data migration? We can't afford downtime."
2. **Training Costs**: "What's the real cost of getting our 500 users trained?"
3. **Vendor Lock-in**: "How do we avoid being trapped in your ecosystem?"
4. **TCO Concerns**: "Your competitor quoted 20% less. Why should we pay more?"
5. **Implementation Timeline**: "Can you really deliver in 6 months?"

### Negotiation Levers
- Multi-year discount (typically 15-25% for 3-year commitment)
- Payment terms (annual vs. monthly, net-30 vs. net-60)
- Implementation services (bundled vs. separate)
- Training credits
- Additional user licenses
- Premium support tier
